# Citizenship System

## Core Principles
- All people are citizens.
- Citizenship is tiered and earned through government service.
- Advancement is voluntary.
- Rights expand with tier; dignity is guaranteed at all tiers.
- Citizenship tiers cannot be inherited, transferred, or purchased.

---

## Citizenship Tiers

Each tier is earned by completing a 4-year government service term.

### Tier 1 Citizen (T1C)
Automatic status.

**Guarantees:**
- Livable baseline:
  - housing (government-owned apartments)
  - food, clothing, utilities
  - healthcare
  - education
  - public transportation
- Full civil liberties
- May work public or private sector
- No voting rights
- No permanent income
- No leadership eligibility

---

### Tier 2 Citizen (T2C)
After 1 term.

**Additional Rights:**
- Permanent government income (lifelong)
- Voting and vote initiation
- City-level leadership eligibility
- Retains all T1C rights

---

### Tier 3 Citizen (T3C)
After 2 terms.

**Additional Rights:**
- County leadership eligibility
- +20% permanent income increase

---

### Tier 4 Citizen (T4C)
After 3 terms.

**Additional Rights:**
- State leadership eligibility
- +20% permanent income increase

---

### Tier 5 Citizen (T5C)
After 4 terms.

**Additional Rights:**
- Federal leadership eligibility
- +20% permanent income increase

---

### Tier 6 Citizen (T6C)
After 5 terms.

**Status:**
- Mandatory retirement (with limited exceptions)
- Final +20% income increase
- Retains all earned rights
- Income ends at death and is non-transferable

---

## Government Service Rules
- Only government-run jobs count.
- Contracted private companies do not qualify.
- Terms pause for uncontrollable interruptions.
- Military service counts.
- Misconduct may revoke tier status.

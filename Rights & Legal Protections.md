Rights & Legal Protections
Foundational Principles

Rights are inherent, not earned.

Citizenship tier expands participation and responsibility, not basic human dignity.

The government exists to:

protect rights

provide baseline stability

prevent concentration of unchecked power

No right is absolute when it directly enables harm, coercion, or loss of due process.

Civil Rights (All Citizens, All Tiers)
Freedom of Speech

Full freedom of expression is guaranteed.

Exception: incitement to action, violence, or coordinated harm.

No punishment for criticism of the government, officials, or systems.

Freedom of Association

Citizens may assemble, organize, and form groups.

Organizations may not:

coerce participation

incite violence

override individual rights

Right to Privacy

Personal data is protected by default.

Access to sensitive data requires:

explicit consent or

professional authorization or

court order

Right to Bear Arms
General Right

Citizens have the right to own and possess arms.

Ownership is regulated, not prohibited.

Requirements

Background check

Mental health evaluation

Mandatory safety training

Proper storage is required

failure to comply may result in revocation of this right

Tiered Access

Higher classes of arms (including explosives) require:

additional checks

increased oversight

stricter storage standards

Registration & Oversight

All weapons must be registered.

A national weapons database records:

weapon identity

ballistic samples

Owner identity is protected

accessible only by court order

Revocation

Weapon rights may be suspended or revoked if:

safety standards are violated

the individual is deemed a credible threat

Revocation follows due process.

Property Rights
Ownership

Citizens may own property and personal assets.

Government-provided baseline housing:

is residency-only

does not constitute ownership

Protection From Seizure

The government may not seize property.

Exception: death with no next of kin.

Property Records

Property ownership is recorded in a protected database.

Access rules:

public verification of ownership

private details restricted

full access requires legal authorization

Legal Due Process
Equality Under the Law

Laws apply equally regardless of:

citizenship tier

income

employment status

Government officials are held to equal or higher standards.

Presumption of Innocence

All citizens are presumed innocent until proven guilty.

Punishment requires conviction.

Fair Trial Rights

Right to:

legal representation

transparent proceedings

appeal

Judges may be removed by public vote.

Data Rights & Legal Databases
Legal Database

Accessible by:

law enforcement

judicial branch

oversight branch

Contains:

criminal records

weapon ballistics (without owner identity by default)

court rulings

Healthcare Database

Access limited to:

the citizen

authorized medical professionals

Law enforcement access requires a court order.

Education & Employment Database

Stores:

subjects

grades

attendance

Students identified by ID number.

Employers access through institutions, not direct contact.

Oversight & Accountability
Oversight Authority

Oversight branch monitors:

all government branches

all government employees

Oversight has no authority over private citizens.

Public Oversight

Citizens may initiate votes to:

remove officials

remove oversight leadership

Oversight is accountable to the people.

Voting Rights & Protections
Eligibility

Voting rights begin at Tier 2 Citizenship.

One vote per citizen per jurisdiction.

Jurisdictional Voting

Citizens may vote in:

cities

counties

states

federal level

Property ownership grants voting rights in that jurisdiction regardless of residency.

Corporate-owned property does not grant voting rights unless owned by a Tier 2+ citizen.

Summary Statement

This rights framework guarantees:

freedom without chaos

security without authoritarianism

transparency without surveillance abuse

ownership without inheritance-based dominance

accountability at every level of power

Rights are protected not by trust alone, but by structure, limitation, and public oversight.
